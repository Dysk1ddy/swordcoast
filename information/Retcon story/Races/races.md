# RACES OF AETHRUNE

Aethrune’s peoples may resemble familiar forms—humans, elves, dwarves—but their origins are deeply tied to the rise and collapse of the Meridian Accord. Each race reflects a different relationship with the old systems, memory, and control.

---

# 1. VALEN (Humans)

## Overview
The most numerous and adaptable race in Aethrune.

## Appearance
- Wide variation in height, build, and complexion
- Practical clothing shaped by region and trade

## Backstory
The Valen were the backbone of the Meridian Accord—workers, traders, soldiers, and citizens. When the system collapsed, they lost structure but not identity.

They became the survivors who rebuilt the world from fragments.

## Cultural Identity
- Highly diverse
- Adaptable and pragmatic
- Often caught between larger powers

---

# 2. ELDREN (Elves)

## Overview
A long-lived, perceptive race tied to memory and fading systems.

## Appearance
- Tall, slender
- Sharp features, luminous eyes
- Ageless presence

## Backstory
The Eldren once served as archivists and observers within the Accord. They maintained records, monitored systems, and ensured continuity.

When the collapse began, they saw it coming—but too late to stop it.

Now they carry fragments of memory others have forgotten.

## Cultural Identity
- Reflective and distant
- Value knowledge and history
- Often burdened by what they remember

---

# 3. DRAEVEN (Dwarves)

## Overview
A resilient, mountain-dwelling people shaped by deep infrastructure.

## Appearance
- Stocky, powerful builds
- Stone-like skin texture
- Thick hair and beards

## Backstory
The Draeven maintained the physical backbone of the Accord—its tunnels, foundations, and deep systems.

When the surface world collapsed, they endured below.

They emerged later to find the world broken—and themselves unchanged.

## Cultural Identity
- Value strength and reliability
- Distrust instability and politics
- Deep respect for craft and structure

---

# 4. HALKIN (Halflings)

## Overview
Small, agile survivors who thrive in unstable conditions.

## Appearance
- Short, light builds
- Expressive features
- Quick movements

## Backstory
The Halkin were messengers, couriers, and small-scale traders during the Accord.

When routes became dangerous, they adapted faster than anyone.

They learned how to survive when systems failed.

## Cultural Identity
- Value freedom and mobility
- Avoid centralized control
- Thrive in uncertain environments

---

# 5. ORUKH (Orcs)

## Overview
A powerful, endurance-focused people shaped by harsh conditions.

## Appearance
- Tall, muscular
- Prominent features, tusk-like teeth
- Green, gray, or earth-toned skin

## Backstory
The Orukh lived on the fringes of the Accord, often excluded from its benefits.

They survived without relying on its systems.

When it collapsed, they were among the least affected—and the most prepared.

## Cultural Identity
- Value strength and independence
- Distrust structured systems
- Strong community bonds

---

# 6. SYLARI (Elven Variant)

## Overview
A rare offshoot of the Eldren, partially out of sync with reality.

## Appearance
- Pale, almost translucent skin
- Subtle visual distortion when moving
- Unfocused or pupil-less eyes

## Backstory
The Sylari emerged from Eldren populations exposed to unstable signal systems.

They perceive patterns others cannot—but at a cost.

## Cultural Identity
- Detached and observant
- Distrust direct perception
- Often isolated

---

# 7. KHYREN (Fire-Blooded)

## Overview
A human-adjacent race infused with internal heat and energy.

## Appearance
- Warm-toned skin
- Faint glow in veins under stress
- Eyes flicker like embers

## Backstory
Descended from populations near energy cores of the Accord.

Their bodies adapted to survive constant exposure.

## Cultural Identity
- Value control and restraint
- Fear uncontrolled power
- Often serve as leaders or enforcers

---

# 8. VAROXI (Forged)

## Overview
A partially mechanical race fused with ancient systems.

## Appearance
- Metallic growths embedded in flesh
- Mechanical joints or plating
- Glowing eyes

## Backstory
Created during catastrophic system failures, when humans and machines merged.

They are living remnants of the Accord itself.

## Cultural Identity
- Struggle with identity
- Feared or mistrusted
- Seek purpose beyond function

---

# 9. THALREN (Riverfolk)

## Overview
A fluid-adapted people tied to water systems.

## Appearance
- Smooth, reflective skin
- Slight webbing between fingers
- Hair flows like water

## Backstory
Descended from those who lived along water-control systems.

They adapted to constant environmental change.

## Cultural Identity
- Value movement and flexibility
- Reject rigid structures
- Live along rivers and coasts

---

# 10. ORYN (Unrecorded)

## Overview
A mysterious and rare people outside recorded systems.

## Appearance
- No consistent traits
- Difficult to remember clearly

## Backstory
The Oryn do not appear in Accord records.

Whether they were missed—or erased—is unknown.

## Cultural Identity
- Undefined even among themselves
- Resist categorization
- Exist outside systems of control

---
