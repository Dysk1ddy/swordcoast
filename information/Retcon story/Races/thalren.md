# THALREN (The Riverborn)

## Overview
The Thalren are a fluid-adapted people shaped by generations living alongside—and within—the water-control systems of the Meridian Accord.

They are not bound to land in the same way as others.

Where most races build permanence, the Thalren embrace **movement, change, and flow**.

To them, stability is not strength.

> Adaptation is.

---

## Physical Appearance

### General Build
- Medium height
- Lean, flexible physiques
- Highly coordinated and balanced movement

### Skin
- Smooth and slightly reflective
- Tones range from:
  - deep blue-gray
  - river-stone green
  - muted teal
- Skin often appears slightly damp or cool to the touch

### Eyes
- Large and expressive
- Colors resemble water conditions:
  - deep blue
  - green
  - murky gray
- Low-light visibility is slightly enhanced

### Hair
- Moves naturally as if influenced by water
- Colors include:
  - dark blue
  - black
  - sea-green
  - pale silver
- Texture ranges from fine to flowing

### Distinctive Traits

#### Webbing
- Subtle webbing between fingers and toes
- Enhances swimming and underwater control

#### Breath Adaptation
- Can hold breath far longer than other races
- Some can perform limited shallow-water breathing

---

## Origins

### Water-System Adaptation Theory (Primary)

The Thalren descend from populations who lived near:

- Meridian water control systems  
- flood regulation channels  
- intake structures and submerged routes  

These systems controlled:
- irrigation
- flow pressure
- reservoir balance

Over generations:
- environmental exposure reshaped physiology
- survival favored flexibility and aquatic adaptation

---

## Physiology

### Aquatic Efficiency
- Exceptional swimmers
- Strong control over buoyancy and movement in water

### Temperature Regulation
- Bodies adapt quickly to cold or damp environments
- Less efficient in extreme dry heat

### Endurance
- Sustained movement over long distances (especially in water)
- Lower burst strength compared to more physically dense races

---

## Cultural Identity

### Core Philosophy: Flow Over Force

> “What does not bend will break.”

---

### Settlements
- Built along rivers, coastlines, or lakes
- Often semi-permanent or mobile
- Structures designed to adapt to shifting water levels

### Social Structure
- Less hierarchical than most races
- Leadership is situational and based on experience
- Decisions often made collectively

### Traditions
- Navigation rites (learning currents and routes)
- Water-marking (leaving signs readable only to Thalren)
- Seasonal movement cycles

---

## Behavior & Personality Tendencies

Common Thalren traits:

- Adaptive and quick-thinking  
- Emotionally fluid and expressive  
- Resistant to rigid structure  
- Comfortable with uncertainty  

They often:
- avoid long-term commitments to fixed systems  
- prefer options over permanence  
- adjust quickly to changing situations  

---

## Social Perception

Other races often see Thalren as:

### Unpredictable
- Hard to pin down in plans or alliances
- Change direction easily

### Free-Spirited
- Not bound by rigid expectations
- Value independence

### Unreliable (Misunderstood)
- Their flexibility is mistaken for lack of commitment

---

## Relationship to Systems

Thalren interact differently with infrastructure:

### Strengths
- Navigate unstable or shifting environments easily
- Less affected by disrupted routes or terrain
- Strong intuition for “flow” systems (water, movement, trade)

### Weaknesses
- Dislike rigid or fixed systems
- Perform poorly in highly structured environments
- Resist long-term system integration

---

## Narrative Role

The Thalren represent:

> Freedom through change.

They exist in contrast to:
- rigid control systems  
- fixed infrastructure  
- enforced stability  

---

## Gameplay Direction (Design Hook)

### Strengths
- Enhanced mobility (especially in water or difficult terrain)  
- Resistance to environmental hazards (flooding, instability)  
- Adaptability to changing conditions  

### Weaknesses
- Reduced effectiveness in rigid or structured systems  
- Lower raw durability compared to heavier races  
- Potential penalties in fixed-position scenarios  

---

## Final Concept

The Thalren do not fight the world as it changes.

They move with it.

> The river does not resist the stone.  
> It outlasts it.
