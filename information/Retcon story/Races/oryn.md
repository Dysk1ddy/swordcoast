# ORYN (The Unrecorded)

## Overview
The Oryn are a distinct and recognizable people—but one that systems, records, and memory struggle to fully contain.

They are present, real, and physical—yet resist being perfectly fixed in place by the world around them.

---

## Physical Appearance

### General Build
- Medium height
- Lean, wiry physiques
- Smooth, deliberate movements

### Skin
- Muted tones: pale ash, bronze, gray-tinted
- Matte texture that absorbs light

### Eyes
- Desaturated irises (gray, faded blue, dull amber)
- Slightly enlarged pupils
- Intense but difficult to maintain eye contact

### Hair
- Typically dark (black, brown, muted silver)
- Straight or slightly wavy

### Distinctive Trait: Recall Drift
People remember Oryn—but never perfectly.
Details blur or shift after interaction.

---

## Origins

### Registry Failure Theory
The Oryn descend from populations whose identities were corrupted or fragmented in Meridian Accord systems.

They are not outside the system.

They are imperfectly inside it.

---

## Relationship to Systems

- Records involving them degrade over time
- Systems misidentify or delay responses
- They are misread, not invisible

---

## Culture

### Communities
- Small, close-knit groups
- Often live on settlement edges

### Naming
- Simple, stable names
- Sometimes multiple contextual names

### Traits
- Value personal relationships over systems
- Independent and observant

---

## Social Perception

- Subtle unease in interactions
- Administrative difficulty (records, contracts)
- Valued for stealth and unpredictability

---

## Narrative Role

The Oryn represent imperfect alignment with controlled systems.

---

## Gameplay Direction

### Strengths
- Harder to track or predict
- Resistant to system-based effects

### Weaknesses
- Reduced benefits from reputation systems
- Difficulty with structured systems

---

## Core Concept

The world almost understands them—but never completely.
