# AETHRUNE — STONEHOLLOW DIG (V1 — FULL LOCATION BIBLE)

## OVERVIEW

**Stonehollow Dig** is the largest active excavation site in the Shatterbelt Frontier and the first place where the surface world directly intersects with intact Meridian structures.

Stonehollow Dig is:
- a wound cut into the earth
- a controlled descent into something older
- a place where knowledge is physically unearthed
- and where the consequences of discovery begin to outweigh the safety of ignorance

Where Emberway teaches control through movement,  
and Greywake teaches control through truth,  

Stonehollow teaches:

> “What happens when you dig too far into something that was never meant to be opened again?”

---

# CORE IDENTITY

Stonehollow exists to answer:

> “What is buried beneath the systems we still depend on?”

It represents:
- the past becoming present
- knowledge becoming risk
- and curiosity becoming consequence

---

# FIRST IMPRESSION

When the player arrives at Stonehollow:

They see:

- a massive excavation pit carved into layered stone
- scaffold systems descending into darkness
- lift platforms moving constantly
- rope systems under strain
- dust clouds rising from below
- workers hauling materials upward
- guards watching downward, not outward

They feel:

- verticality
- exposure
- pressure from below instead of around
- that something important has already been uncovered
- and something worse is still deeper

---

# MACRO STRUCTURE

Stonehollow is organized vertically, not horizontally.

## 1. SURFACE RING

### Features
- command tents
- supply depots
- intake checkpoints
- worker barracks
- defensive perimeter

### Purpose
- staging and logistics

### Tone
Busy, controlled, optimistic but strained

---

## 2. UPPER EXCAVATION LAYER

### Features
- carved stone terraces
- tool stations
- scaffold bridges
- partial tunnel openings

### Purpose
- initial dig work
- material sorting

### Tone
Labor-driven, loud, grounded

---

## 3. MID DESCENT

### Features
- rope lifts
- suspended platforms
- narrow walkways
- support beams
- visible depth below

### Purpose
- access deeper layers

### Tone
Dangerous, unstable, transitional

---

## 4. LOWER EXCAVATION

### Features
- exposed Meridian stone
- geometric cuts unlike natural rock
- embedded structures
- sealed doorways

### Purpose
- active discovery zone

### Tone
Unnatural, quiet, heavy

---

## 5. INNER STRUCTURE ENTRY

### Features
- partially opened chambers
- intact walls
- strange surfaces
- no visible wear

### Purpose
- true system interaction

### Tone
Oppressive, alien, controlled

- failure risk

Use:
- traversal + hazard

---

## 2. THE FRACTURE LINE

A crack exposing deeper layers.

- unnatural symmetry
- heat or resonance sometimes felt

Use:
- foreshadowing deeper systems

---

## 3. THE TOOL FIELD

Area where broken tools accumulate.

- tools fail faster here
- materials degrade inconsistently

Meaning:
environment resists excavation

First exposed internal room.

- no echo
- smooth surfaces
- no debris

Use:
- first true “system space” moment

- not fully understood

# HISTORY

## MERIDIAN ERA

Stonehollow was likely:
- part of a subsurface network
- a node in a larger structure
- not meant for surface access

## COLLAPSE

Surface sealed.
Knowledge lost.
Location forgotten.

## REDISCOVERY

Initial dig attempts found:
- unusual stone patterns
- unnatural geometry

Excavation expanded rapidly.

## CURRENT STATE

Dig is ongoing but increasingly dangerous.
