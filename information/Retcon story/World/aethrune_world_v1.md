# AETHRUNE  
## World Development — Broad Scale (V1)

---

# CORE CONCEPT

Aethrune is not a world ruled by kings.

It is a world ruled by **systems that still function after their creators are gone**.

Roads, records, waterworks, signal towers, and buried infrastructure still shape reality.

Most people believe they are rebuilding civilization.

> In truth, they are living inside the remains of one.

---

# THE AGE BEFORE — THE MERIDIAN ACCORD

## What It Was

The Meridian Accord was a **total system of control and coordination** built to eliminate uncertainty.

It unified:

- Trade routes  
- Resource distribution  
- Communication systems  
- Population tracking  
- Decision modeling  

It did not simply govern people.

> It governed outcomes.

---

## How It Worked

The Accord functioned through three layers:

### 1. Physical Layer — Infrastructure
- Roads that optimized travel routes  
- Water systems that controlled supply and flow  
- Transit networks connecting all major regions  

### 2. Informational Layer — Records
- Identity registries  
- Trade logs  
- Predictive archives  
- Behavioral tracking  

### 3. Predictive Layer — Pattern Systems
- Systems that learned from decisions  
- Models that anticipated outcomes  
- Routing that adjusted based on behavior  

---

## The Collapse

The fall of the Meridian Accord is the defining mystery of Aethrune.

Known facts:

- Systems began to contradict each other  
- Records became inconsistent  
- Routes led to unintended destinations  
- Infrastructure failed in cascading patterns  

Unknown:

- Was it sabotage?  
- System overload?  
- Emergent failure?  
- Or something deeper?

---

# THE CURRENT AGE — THE FRACTURED WORLD

Aethrune exists in a **partial state of recovery**.

The world is not destroyed—but incomplete.

---

## Key Characteristics

### 1. Systems Still Function (Partially)
- Roads still guide movement  
- Old facilities still operate  
- Signals still travel  

But:
- Not reliably  
- Not consistently  
- Not safely  

---

### 2. Knowledge Is Fragmented
- No one fully understands the old world  
- Records contradict each other  
- Truth depends on who controls it  

---

### 3. Control Is Localized
- No global authority  
- Power exists in regions, factions, and networks  

---

### 4. The World Is Layered

There are effectively **three overlapping worlds**:

1. The visible world (settlements, travel, survival)  
2. The systemic world (routes, infrastructure, control)  
3. The hidden world (signals, prediction, deeper systems)  

---

# GEOGRAPHY OF AETHRUNE

The continent is divided not by nations—but by **functional regions**.

---

## 1. The Shatterbelt Frontier (Primary Setting)

A broken region where the game begins.

### Features
- Collapsed route networks  
- Unstable settlements  
- Heavy faction conflict  

### Importance
- Entry point into the deeper system  
- Where control of movement is contested  

---

## 2. The Vein of Glass (Subterranean Region)

A network of underground systems and ruins.

### Features
- Excavation sites  
- Water control systems  
- Signal distortion zones  

### Importance
- Where information becomes unstable  
- Where factions begin to understand the system  

---

## 3. The Meridian Depths

The buried core of the Accord.

### Features
- Massive infrastructure hubs  
- Active system networks  
- Reality-distorting phenomena  

### Importance
- Source of system control  
- Endgame location  

---

## 4. Peripheral Regions (Future Expansion)

### The Stormreach Coast
- Violent weather patterns
- Damaged signal towers affecting the skies

### The Iron Spine Mountains
- Deep structural systems
- Draeven strongholds and ancient machinery

### The Hollow Expanse
- Areas where systems failed completely
- Minimal records, unstable reality

# THE PEOPLE OF AETHRUNE

Civilization is not unified.

It is **fragmented into functional groups**.

---

## Settlements

- Built around:
  - trade routes  
  - water access  
  - old infrastructure  

- Types:
  - fortified towns  
  - mobile caravan hubs  
  - ruin-adjacent settlements  

---

## Travel

Travel is dangerous—but necessary.

- Roads may:
  - shift  
  - degrade  
  - lead to unexpected outcomes  

Controlling routes = controlling power.

---

## Knowledge

Information is currency.

- Maps are unreliable  
- Records can be altered  
- Truth is contested
