## Kaelis Starling (Astral Elf Ranger)

### Early Life
Kaelis grew up on the edge of cultivated land, where farms turned into wilderness without warning. His mother taught him survival; his father—absent but mythologized—left him with stories of precision, patience, and detachment. Kaelis internalized both, but never fully trusted either worldview.

### Defining Trauma
The failed caravan wasn’t just a job—it was his first independent contract. He *knew* the ambush was coming. He hesitated because he couldn’t prove it. Survivors later told him: “You were right. You just weren’t fast enough.”

### Personality
- Speaks in short, precise observations
- Watches hands more than faces
- Sleeps lightly; wakes before others
- Rarely laughs, but when he does, it’s sudden and brief

### Interaction Hooks
- Warns player before ambushes (sometimes subtly)
- Gains trust if player acts on instinct
- Loses trust if player hesitates excessively

### Personal Quest: “Echoes of the Road”
1. Rumors of survivors from the failed caravan
2. Discovery: ambush was *set up*, not random
3. Confrontation with orchestrator (ties into Ashen Brand)
4. Resolution:
   - Kill them → Kaelis becomes colder, more decisive
   - Expose them → Kaelis regains moral balance
   - Let them go → Kaelis questions player judgment

## Rhogar Valeguard (Forged Paladin)

### Early Life
Raised within a structured order tied to caravan protection, Rhogar was taught that order prevents chaos. His entire identity was built around discipline, hierarchy, and oathkeeping.

### Breaking Point
He discovered his order was selectively protecting caravans—prioritizing profit over lives. When he intervened against orders, he was branded disobedient.

### Personality
- Formal speech, even in casual settings
- Struggles with humor
- Takes watch shifts seriously, even off-duty
- Visibly uncomfortable with deceit

### Interaction Hooks
- Approves of lawful, transparent decisions
- Disapproves of manipulation or secrecy
- Will directly challenge player decisions

### Personal Quest: “The Weight of Oath”
1. Encounter former order agents
2. Discover they are still operating under corrupt directives
3. Forced choice:
   - Reinforce the system (restore order)
   - Break it publicly (cause instability)
   - Reform it quietly (middle path, hardest)

### Special Dynamic
- With Bryn: escalating tension over truth vs. concealment
- With Elira: philosophical debates on justice vs. mercy

## Tolan Ironshield (Dwarf Fighter)

### Early Life
Born into a clan that prized formation combat, Tolan trained his entire life to fight as part of a unit—not as an individual.

### The Broken Line
His company’s collapse wasn’t just defeat—it was chaos. Orders failed. Someone panicked. Someone fled. Tolan *broke formation* to survive.

### Personality
- Direct, blunt, practical
- Drinks slowly, not heavily
- Watches group positioning constantly
- Prefers simple, reliable plans

### Interaction Hooks
- Approves of defensive, steady tactics
- Dislikes risky or “clever” gambits
- Gains trust when player protects party members

### Personal Quest: “Hold the Line”
1. Evidence surfaces about the failed battle
2. Survivors give conflicting accounts
3. Truth revealed:
   - Someone betrayed the line OR
   - The system itself failed
4. Resolution:
   - Accept truth → emotional closure
   - Deny truth → remains hardened
   - Rewrite narrative → becomes leader figure

## Bryn Underbough (Halfling Rogue)

### Early Life
Grew up in transient trade camps, learning early that information is currency. Joined a smuggling network not out of greed—but survival.

### Breaking Point
Realized her routes enabled violent raids. She didn’t *see* the consequences until it was too late.

### Personality
- Fast-talking, deflective humor
- Constantly scanning exits
- Avoids direct emotional conversations
- Fidgets with tools or coins

### Interaction Hooks
- Approves of clever, indirect solutions
- Dislikes rigid moral judgments
- Gains trust when player protects her secrets

### Personal Quest: “Loose Ends (Expanded)”
1. Ledger fragments found
2. Old contacts resurface
3. Ledger is more than routes—it’s influence networks
4. Resolution:
   - Burn it → freedom, but loss of power
   - Sell it → wealth, but moral decay
   - Expose it → chaos, but justice

### Special Dynamic
- With Rhogar: ideological conflict
- With Kaelis: mutual understanding of “reading danger”

## Elira Dawnmantle (Human Cleric)

### Early Life
Raised in a temple that emphasized hope, but stationed in harsh frontier regions where hope often failed.

### Crisis of Faith
Saw prayers unanswered during a plague outbreak. Since then, she believes action matters more than faith alone.

### Personality
- Warm but intense
- Makes eye contact when speaking seriously
- Prays quietly, not ceremonially
- Carries small tokens from those she’s helped

### Interaction Hooks
- Approves of protecting innocents
- Struggles with morally gray decisions
- Gains trust when player shows compassion

### Personal Quest: “Faith Under Ash (Expanded)”
1. Captured zealot encounter
2. Discovery: zealot is not entirely wrong
3. Choice:
   - Mercy → strengthens hope
   - Execution → reinforces safety
   - Conversion attempt → risky, complex outcome

### Special Dynamic
- With Rhogar: debates justice vs. mercy
- With Tolan: grounding presence

## Party Interaction System (Expanded)

### Dynamic Banter Triggers
- After combat victories → Kaelis + Bryn exchange observations
- After moral decisions → Elira reacts, Rhogar counters
- During rest → Tolan shares stories (low chance unless trust high)

### Conflict Escalation System
- Certain decisions increase tension between companions
- At thresholds:
  - Argument scene triggers
  - Player must intervene or choose side
  - Outcomes affect long-term loyalty

### Trust-Based Dialogue Evolution
- Neutral → surface-level dialogue
- Good → personal stories unlock
- Great → vulnerability + unique abilities
- Exceptional → companions defend player decisions automatically

## Nim Ardentglass (Unrecorded Wizard) [ADDED TO V2]

### Early Life
Nim was raised among Pact archivists who treated maps like sacred objects. From a young age, he learned that knowledge wasn’t just power—it was responsibility.

### Defining Event
During a deep survey beneath the Sword Mountains, his entire team vanished without signs of struggle. The only thing left behind was a map that *changed* when he wasn’t looking.

### Personality
- Talks rapidly when anxious
- Obsessively documents everything
- Avoids silence at all costs
- Laughs when stressed

### Interaction Hooks
- Approves of investigative decisions
- Gains trust when player values knowledge
- Distrusts blind destruction of unknown forces

### Personal Quest: “The Living Map” (V2 Integration)
1. Discover unstable mapping anomalies
2. Confirm maps react to observation
3. Resolution:
   - Study → gain insight, increase danger
   - Destroy → lose knowledge, reduce risk
   - Balance → unstable hybrid path

## Irielle Ashwake (Fire-Blooded Warlock) [ADDED TO V2]

### Early Life
Irielle was raised within a Quiet Choir-adjacent community where certain individuals were trained to “listen” to something deeper beneath reality.

### Defining Event
She didn’t escape the Choir—she was deliberately released, though she doesn’t know why.

### Personality
- Speaks carefully and slowly
- Avoids direct answers
- Intense eye contact
- Emotionally guarded

### Interaction Hooks
- Approves of cautious, controlled decisions
- Distrusts reckless curiosity
- Reacts strongly to anything related to the Choir

### Personal Quest: “The Quiet Voice” (V2 Integration)
1. Encounters with Choir remnants
2. Realization she is still connected
3. Resolution:
   - Sever → lose power, gain autonomy
   - Embrace → gain power, risk corruption
   - Balance → unstable path
